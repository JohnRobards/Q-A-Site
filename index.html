Welcome to Washu!


Just starting out at Washington University in St. Louis and not sure of what to do, where to eat, how to meet people with similar interests or what classes you should take? What about what you're going to do with your life or any of the other myriad concerns you might have above living in a new place, possibly for the first time? Well, you're in luck as we hope this FAQ can be a comprehensive list of any questions our new students may have. Think we missed something? Please scroll down to the bottom of the page and either give us a call or submit your question using the button found there for an answer via email.


Washington University in St. Louis strives to be an open and accepting community. One that is built on trust, kindness, and learning. We accept people with a wide variety of interests to our University in the hopes that everyone is able to find their own niche and be able to enjoy themselves while they learn. Whether you're browsing this FAQ because you're a new freshman, foreign exchange student, transfer student, or were just curious about some aspect of life on campus that you didn't know much about, we promise to do our best to address your concerns and help. No matter what your interests or worries are, WashU is here to help, so don't hesitate to ask us or your classmates anything. We all love to help!


New Student FAQ


How do I decide what courses to take?

What courses you wish to take are entirely up to you! Taking a mix of courses that you might already be somewhat familiar with and some that you've always been interested in but never had the chance to study before is usually a good idea. It is not necessary to declare your major until second semester sophomore year and new students are encouraged to experiment and figure out what it is that they want to study. If you want specific course suggestions, the best places to check are with your four year advisor (they're all quite lovely and would absolutely love to talk to you), your RA's, your WUSAs, and to ask anyone you may already know at the school for their suggestions.

For a complete list of possible courses, go here: Courses

If you're confused about how to register or what Webstac is, go here: Washu Tools


I took AP courses in highschool or I wish to transfer credits from another university, what do those count for?

For the majority of AP courses (other than a few language courses), a score requirement of 4 or 5 is required to receive credit for the class. A maximum of 15 credits, through transfer or testing scores, may be counted towards your degree. If you feel you qualify, call 1-888-CALL-4-AP (with course code 6929) to submit your test scores. If you wish you submit another type of test score (such as the IB) or to transfer credits, please follow the link below to read our full policy.

If you want to read our full policy, go here: Transfer Policy


Where can I find a group of people who like to do "X" activity?

Clubs: Clubs are always a great place to start to find people with similar interests to yours, and WashU has clubs for almost every type of activity a college student might be interested in. Our club topics range from political activism to a club dedicated solely to Texas Hold'em to almost anything else you could imagine. Go the the link below for a full list, or just wait a few weeks into the semester for the Club Fair, where you can go around and talk to representatives of all of these great clubs and sign up! If you're joining late into the semester and still want to join a club, your best bet would be to go to the link below and send any clubs that you're interested in an email directly or to ask your advisor/RA for club suggestions. Remember to not be afraid to sign up for a lot of clubs, they're a great way to meet people and you don't have to go if you don't like it after the first meeting.

Club Sports: If you were specifically looking for a sport that you wanted to play and meet some friends along the way, then the place to go would be here. WashU's club sports offer a nice way to compete in a lowkey environment and meet new friends with similar interests. Don't be afraid if you're not very good either, most people who do club sports are just looking to have fun and meet new people. All club sports also will have teams for new students or freshman, so you'll be able to meet a lot of other students in a similar situation to you!

All currently active student groups at Washu: Clubs


Where can I eat on or around campus?

On Campus: As far as dining on campus goes, there are a variety of places to eat, including Bear's Den, The DUC, Ibby's, Stanley's, Subway, The Village and a variety of coffee/snack locations spread around campus. For a breakdown of these places and their offerings in detail, please go to the page linked below. All of these locations accept meal points, which every student at WashU has as part of their meal plan, so all students have easy access to food and don't need to carry cash or credit cards around.

Off Campus: As for dining off campus, that really depends on how far you're willing to walk/ride the metro or if you know someone with access to a car (unlikely if you're a freshman or any new student coming in). The loop is within walking distance and has a lot of food options if you get bored of the stuff on campus. As for other places, this is a pretty good list of some of the best places around St. Louis to get you started. If you want any further suggestions, the best way to find out is to ask upperclassmen or your advisor where they like to eat around St. Louis. The metro is safe and it's easy to get to a variety of places using it.

The dining services website, for up to date menus and food options: Food

Descriptions of the offerings at the variety of food places on the main campus: Restaurants

How do I make friends?

Clubs are a great way to meet people, refer back to the question above for more details on that. Other than that, just try talking to people. Go up to people on your floor and ask what their interests are, or what classes they're taking and see if you have any common interests. Most new students are just as scared as you are, so don't worry about freaking them out. Or if you have time before one of your classes start strike up a conversation with the person sitting next to you instead of playing around on your phone. We at WashU strive to make all of our students feel at home, so if you're having any issues please let us know right away at the first year center, where our goal is to make sure every new student is happy.

If you're having issues meeting people with similar interests: Contact Us
